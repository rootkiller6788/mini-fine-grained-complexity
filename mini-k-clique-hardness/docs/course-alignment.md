# Course Alignment: mini-k-clique-hardness

## k-Clique Hardness in Nine-School Curriculum

### MIT
- **6.841 Advanced Complexity Theory**: Fine-grained complexity, ETH/SETH
- **6.046/18.410 Design & Analysis of Algorithms**: NP-completeness proofs
  including k-Clique via 3SAT reduction
- Coverage: SETH lower bounds, color-coding, parameterized lower bounds

### Stanford
- **CS254 Computational Complexity**: ETH, sub-exponential time, 
  parameterized complexity
- **CS264 Beyond Worst-Case Analysis**: Planted clique, average-case
- Coverage: Conditional lower bounds methodology

### Berkeley
- **CS278 Computational Complexity**: W-hierarchy, parameterized
  complexity, completeness proofs
- Coverage: W[1]-completeness of k-Clique

### CMU
- **15-855 Graduate Complexity**: Fine-grained complexity, k-Clique
  lower bounds, equivalence classes
- Coverage: n^{k-o(1)} lower bound from SETH

### Princeton
- **COS 522 Computational Complexity**: ETH/SETH conjectures,
  parameterized complexity
- **COS 551 Advanced Complexity**: Conditional lower bounds
- Coverage: ETH->n^{Omega(k)} mapping

### Caltech
- **CS 151 Complexity Theory**: NP-completeness, graph problems
- **CS 154 Limits of Computation**: SETH and fine-grained analysis
- Coverage: Fundamental k-Clique complexity

### Cambridge
- **Part II Complexity Theory**: Parameterized complexity, W-hierarchy
- **Part III Advanced Complexity**: Fine-grained complexity theory
- Coverage: Downey & Fellows framework

### Oxford
- **Computational Complexity**: FPT algorithms, color-coding
- **Advanced Complexity Theory**: Conditional lower bounds
- Coverage: Alon-Yuster-Zwick color-coding

### ETH Zurich
- **263-4650 Advanced Complexity**: SETH-based lower bounds
- **252-0400 Logic & Computation**: Circuit complexity, weft
- Coverage: Circuit weft analysis for W[1]
